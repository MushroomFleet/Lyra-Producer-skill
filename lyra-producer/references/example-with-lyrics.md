# Example Songbook That Never Was
*A tiny sample catalogue showing that lyrics and a timed structure are OPTIONAL and per-track. Use it as a template — and as a fixture the CLI can dry-run.*

---

## How this catalogue works
- The `###` heading is the only track boundary.
- The music **prompt** is always the fenced code block under the heading.
- **Lyrics** and **Timed structure** are optional — include them only when a track needs them, under a `**Lyrics**` / `**Timed structure**` label (fenced or plain).

---

## The Catalogue

### Instrumental Opener — *"First Light"*
> No words, no structure — just a prompt. Most tracks look like this.

```
Warm ambient solo piano, slow and unhurried, gentle rubato, felt piano close-miked with a soft room. Instrumental only, no vocals.
```

### Ballad No. 3 — *"Letters to the Sea"*
> A full song: prompt + lyrics + a timed shape.

```
Melancholic indie-folk ballad, intimate female lead vocal, fingerpicked acoustic guitar and soft strings, warm and close. Moderate tempo, building gently.
```

**Lyrics**
```
[Verse]
I wrote your name in the wet grey sand
knowing the tide would take it back
[Chorus]
but the sea remembers, the sea remembers
everything we never said
```

**Timed structure**
```
[0:00 - 0:18] Intro: fingerpicked guitar alone, no vocals
[0:18 - 1:05] Verse 1
[1:05 - 1:45] Chorus, strings enter
[1:45 - 2:30] Verse 2, fuller arrangement
```

### Anthem — *"Hold the Line"*
> Lyrics can also be plain text under a `#### Lyrics` sub-heading — no fence required.

```
Uplifting stadium-rock anthem, male lead vocal with gang backing vocals, driving drums and bright electric guitars, big and euphoric. Fast and energetic.
```

#### Lyrics
[Verse]
We were told to fold, we did not fold
[Chorus]
hold the line, hold the line
till the morning breaks and the dark resigns
